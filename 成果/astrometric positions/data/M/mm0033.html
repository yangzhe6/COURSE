Comments.
  1. Some observations of Phobos presented here are also available in mm0006 where, however, relative coordinates are given.
  2. The rms residuals were obtained by comparing satellites' positions with the JPL ephemerides.
     DE403 was used for Mars positions.

Format.
  1. Satellite index (n: 1 - Phobos, 2 - Deimos)
  2. Year  of the date of observation
  3. Month of the date of observation
  4. Day   of the date of observation with decimals
  5. Hours   of right ascension (alpha, h)
  6. Minutes of right ascension (alpha, m)
  7. Seconds of right ascension with decimals (alpha, s)
  8. Degrees of declination (delta, deg)
  9. Minutes of declination (delta, ')
 11. Seconds of declination with decimals (delta, '')
 12. Plate number (pl)
 13. Number of exposures on the plate (exp)
 14. Observer code (obs: 1 - Major, 2 - Semerenko, 12 - Major and Semerenko)
 15. Number of reference stars (rs)
 16. Residual (O-C)alpha (arcsec) (99.9 if not given)
 17. Residual (O-C)delta (arcsec) (99.9 if not given)
 18. Measuring machine codes (mm: 1 - TO ASCORECORD 3 DP; 2 - MAO ASCOREMAT, 12 - both machines)
