Comments.
    The data of observation are given as X, Y cordinates relative to 
    the Pluto's absolute reference position given by Right ascension (RA) 
    and delination DEC. Thus the satellite coordinetes are given 
    for absolute position of sateliite. The Pluto's reference position  
    is explaned in the relevant paper (see Reference).
    Here X=Delta(alpha}*cos(delta), X=Delta(delta} whre
    Delta(alpha} is difference in Right ascension between satelite 
    and Pluto's reference position, and Delta(delta} is
    difference in Declination (delta is declination of the
.   Pluto's reference position).

Format.
  1. Year of the date of observation
  2. Month of the date of observation
  3. Day of the date of observation 
  4. Hour (h) of the date of observation
  5. Minute (m( of the date of observation 
  6. Seconds (s) of the date of observation with decimals (UTC)
  7. Number of the satellite (N sat)
  8. X, arcsec
  9. error of X, arcsec
 10. Y, arcsec
 11. error of Y, arcsec
 12. Right ascension (RA) of the Pluto's absolute reference position, deg,
 11. Declination (DEC) of the Pluto's absolute reference position, deg,
