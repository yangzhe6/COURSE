Comments.
  1. The mutual distance s (in arcseconds) between Charon and Pluto can be obtained as:
\begin{equation*} s=m\times\sqrt{(X_C-X_P)^2+(Y_C-Y_P)^2} \end{equation*} 
     where: m = 0.1016 arcsec/pixel is the scale for the WFC,
           (XP, YP) and (XC, YC) are pixel coordinates of Pluto and Charon on the CCD frame (see the values X_P, Y_P, X_C, Y_C in the data).
  2. Charon's position angle P can be obtained as:
\begin{equation*} P=P_{axis}-225^\circ+\frac{180^\circ}{\pi}\arctan\left(\frac{Y_C-Y_P}{X_C-X_P}\right) \end{equation*} 
     where Paxis is the WFC roll angle (see the value P_axis in the data) measured in degrees. 
  3. Values for s and P obtained in this way are given in the data file.

Format.
  1. Year of the date of observation
  2. Month of the date of observation
  3. Day of the date of observation 
  4. Hour (h) of the date of observation
  5. Minute (m) of the date of observation 
  6. Seconds (s) of the date of observation with decimals (UTC)
  7. Geocentric x-coordinate of the HST (X, km)
  8. Geocentric y-coordinate of the HST (Y, km)
  9. Geocentric z-coordinate of the HST (Z, km)
 10. x-coordinate of Pluto in the image (X_P, pixels)
 11. y-coordinate of Pluto in the image (Y_P, pixels)
 12. x-coordinate of Charon in the image (X_C, pixels)
 13. y-coordinate of Charon in the image (Y_C, pixels)
 14. WFC roll angle, i.e. position angle of the HST's V3 axis (P_axis, degrees)
 15. The mutual apparent topocentric (HST) distance Charon-Pluto, s, arcsec
 16. The mutual apparent topocentric (HST) position angle of Charon relative to Pluto, P, degrees
